Tarjei Vesaas, The Horse, 2026/03/28

The day was hot and long–now it’s going.
Evening: the sounds are few and far between.
A little girl pads into the room naked: Goodnight to father!
She smacks a kiss, and goes off to her cool pillow.\

He stays. Quiets down. At a messy table.
Paper books. Written and printed words.
The girl’s kiss on his cheeck was warm and round,
but nonetheless, forgotten in a moment.\

Now it’s work. And time can run by as it pleases.
At length he look towards the window–and startles:
It’s dark out. That’s the best time for work.
–But now there’s a face against the window–a horse!.\

A huge horse’s face. Gray as clay.
With black deep eyes. That’s all you see.
And not the slightest movement. But the whole pane is filled.
–A shadowy, vanished time returns, unreels.\

He stares at the vision. Yes! the horse is gray.
This one had many weary days to look back on
when he was shot behind the fence. With bent knees
he lay overturned, in a drone of horseflies. Father stood by, moved.\

Around this gray horse everything was sweat and toil.
Wagon-clatter, broiling sun, smell of hay, rain, slogging through snow.
The rein rasped a young hand. Which learned the trick
to guiding, in all seasons, a horse that trudged and trudged.\

–And now he’s come to the window. And calls up,
enchanted, all that was rich and simple.
Ah, no, it’s not for fun. He hasn’t come in peace.
The mute question bears down: What is it that you do?\

What are you doing at this table? Are you ready
to meet the child, as you were, with everything that you are?
It cuts through words and paper. It seeps down.
to what’s underneath: the core of him.\

What can he answer? Everything’s waiting to hear.
He looks toward the dark eyes, and fear comes sailing like clouds:
His own wide and precious childhood world
appeared with the horse, and now it closes in.\

Then meets the simplest part of this world: Father, goodnight!
And the pressure of lips on his cheek seems beyond doubt.
Bless the girl who radiates strength from her pillow.
The horse can stay where he is with his question: shut out.\

Tr. Roger Greenwald
